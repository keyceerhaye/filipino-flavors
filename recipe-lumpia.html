Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1 medium onion, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup carrots, julienned
  • 1 cup cabbage, shredded
  • 1/2 cup green beans, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 20 pieces lumpia wrappers
  • 1 egg, beaten (for sealing)
  • Cooking oil for frying
  • Sweet and sour sauce for dipping

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pan over medium heat. Add the ground pork and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon.
  2. Add the minced garlic and onion. Cook until fragrant and onions are translucent.
  3. Add the carrots, cabbage, and green beans. Stir-fry for 3-5 minutes until vegetables are slightly tender but still crisp.
  4. Season with soy sauce, salt, and black pepper. Mix well and cook for another 2 minutes. Remove from heat and let cool completely.
  5. Lay a lumpia wrapper on a flat surface. Place about 2 tablespoons of filling near one end of the wrapper.
  6. Fold the sides inward, then roll tightly from the end with the filling. Seal the edge with beaten egg.
  7. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.
  8. Heat oil in a deep pan over medium-high heat. Fry the lumpia in batches until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  9. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  10. Serve hot with sweet and sour sauce. Enjoy your crispy Filipino spring rolls!
