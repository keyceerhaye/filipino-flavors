Ingredients

  • 2 lbs oxtail, cut into pieces
  • 1 lb tripe (optional)
  • 1/2 cup peanut butter
  • 1/4 cup ground rice (toasted and ground)
  • 1 large onion, sliced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups eggplant, sliced
  • 1 bundle string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 bundle bok choy or pechay
  • 2-3 tablespoons annatto seeds (achuete)
  • 6-8 cups beef stock or water
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)
  • Salt to taste
  • Shrimp paste (bagoong) for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, boil the oxtail and tripe in water until very tender (about 1.5-2 hours). Reserve the stock.
  2. In a small pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and add annatto seeds. Cook until the oil turns red, then strain and discard seeds. Set aside the colored oil.
  3. In the same large pot, heat the annatto oil. Saut√© garlic and onion until fragrant.
  4. Add the cooked oxtail and tripe. Pour in the reserved stock and bring to a boil.
  5. Dissolve the ground rice in a small amount of water, then add to the pot. Stir continuously to avoid lumps.
  6. Add peanut butter and stir until well incorporated. Simmer for 10-15 minutes until the sauce thickens.
  7. Add fish sauce and season with salt. Adjust the thickness by adding more stock if needed.
  8. In a separate pot, blanch the vegetables (eggplant, string beans, bok choy) until just tender.
  9. Add the blanched vegetables to the kare-kare pot and cook for 2-3 more minutes.
  10. Serve hot over steamed rice with shrimp paste (bagoong) on the side. Enjoy this rich and creamy Filipino stew!
